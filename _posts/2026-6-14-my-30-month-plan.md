# My 30-Month Plan

## Who I am

Hi, I’m Xuze Chen. I recently graduated from the University of Edinburgh, where I studied Applied Mathematics. I’m now preparing to start a master’s degree in Analysis at UC Berkeley.

This site is a place for me to write through a transition I’m still figuring out: from applied mathematics into robot learning, with a particular interest in reinforcement learning-based whole-body control. I’ll use it to keep notes, record mistakes, organize what I learn, and think out loud along the way.

I’m mainly writing for myself. But I also hope these posts may be useful to someone who comes from a similar background, wants to move toward robotics or RL, and is not quite sure where to begin.

## The three phases

I have a rough plan for the next thirty months, though I expect it to change as I learn more.

The first part, from June to December 2026, is about building the basics. This summer I’ll focus on coding and reinforcement learning, while also reviewing linear algebra, probability, and ODEs for the control side of robotics. Once I get to Berkeley, I want to use Berkeley Humanoid Lite as my main hands-on project. My goal is to work through the pipeline carefully enough that I can leave behind a clean repo, training notes, results, and a short demo video.

The next step is to get involved in research more seriously, ideally by joining a lab as an RA. I want to spend the beginning of that experience learning how the group actually works: how people read papers, design experiments, debug systems, and decide what is worth pursuing. I’m currently interested in whole-body control, safety constraints, and sim-to-real, but I’m also trying to stay open-minded until I have more real research experience.

Over time, I hope the RA work can help me become less of a beginner and more of an independent researcher. I want to learn how to ask better questions, contribute to a project in a meaningful way, and, if possible, help write a paper. More broadly, I want these next two and a half years to tell me whether a PhD is the right next step, and to prepare myself well if it is.
